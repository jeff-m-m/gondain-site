GONDAINGondain's Observational Notes on Damage, Auras, Inventory & Nameds

Read-only. GONDAIN never touches the game — it reads the log file EverQuest already writes.

What the one eye watches

Damage

Live DPS windows, a real-time sparkline, per-fight ability breakdowns with charts, group parses, and death recaps.

Auras

Buff tracking that learns spell durations by watching your casts — expiry warnings, watched-buff alarms, spoken alerts.

Inventory

A personal drop database: every item you loot, which mob dropped it, in which zone, at what rate. Plus a session loot ledger.

Nameds

Rare-creature detection from con messages, kill logs with observed drop percentages, and respawn-window countdowns.

Seven tabs

COMBATThe live dashboard: DPS, fights, buffs, abilities, stance, session economy.
SKY QUESTSPlane of Sky class quests with automatic loot counting, item stats, and class-unlock tracking.
LOOTEverything looted and sold this session, newest first, with coin rates.

ITEMSSearch your drop history by item, zone, or mob — including per-zone loot tables.

HISTORYParses over time, damage per day, filters for range, raid bosses, victories, and outliers.

How it works

  1. Turn on logging in game with /log on — EverQuest writes everything that happens to a text file.
  2. Point GONDAIN at the log. It replays your history to learn your buffs, quests, nameds, and drops, then follows along live.
  3. Play. Every kill, loot, buff, and raid lockout is tracked automatically and kept in your own local data files — trimming the log never loses your catalog.

The name

Gondain was a gray-haired, one-eyed Dwarf — an original EverQuest character rolled in 1999. The name comes from Tolkien's Silmarillion: gond, stone. A quarter century later the old dwarf keeps watch again, one good eye on the log file, missing nothing.

GONDAIN is a fan-made, read-only log companion for EverQuest Legends.
